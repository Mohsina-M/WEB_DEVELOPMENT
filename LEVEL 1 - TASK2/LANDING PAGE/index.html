FEATURED BOOKS

"To Kill a Mockingbird"

Author: Harper Lee

Harper Lee's Pulitzer Prize-winning novel explores racial injustice and moral growth in the American South. Set in the 1930s, it follows the trial of a black man accused of raping a white woman, seen through the eyes of a young girl named Scout Finch.

"Hamlet"

Author: William Shakespeare

Hamlet, a seminal tragedy by Shakespeare, explores existentialism, morality, and human complexity. Prince Hamlet, mourning his father's death, confronts despair and suspicion after discovering his murder by his uncle Claudius, triggering a quest for revenge.

"The Catcher in the Rye"

Author: J.D. Salinger

The Catcher in the Rye narrates the story of Holden Caulfield, a disillusioned teenager navigating the complexities of adolescence and society. The book is renowned for its candid portrayal of teenage angst and alienation.

"The Great Gatsby"

Author: F. Scott Fitzgerald

F. Scott Fitzgerald's classic novel set in the Roaring Twenties explores themes of the American Dream, love, and the corrupting influence of wealth through the enigmatic Jay Gatsby and his pursuit of love and wealth.
